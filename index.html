Research Architecture

Perception, Intelligence, and Action

Neuroide is organized around three research pillars. Each pillar contains topic programs that define how the lab studies measurement, modeling, reasoning, control, validation, and deployment.

The lab treats autonomy as a systems problem rather than a stack of disconnected specialties. Reliable behavior begins in perception, matures through intelligence, and only counts when it survives action under real constraints, uncertainty, and failure modes.

Perception

Signals, multimodal sensing, physiological systems, and the conversion of noisy measurement into structured observations.

Intelligence

Machine learning, generative and reasoning systems, representation, inference, and uncertainty-aware model building.

Action

Robotics, control and planning, validation and simulation, and systems that execute under real operational pressure.
