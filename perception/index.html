Research Pillar / Perception

Perception begins where noisy measurements become usable observations.

Perception is the research pillar concerned with how the world, the body, and technical environments are measured, modeled, and made available for later inference. Neuroide treats perception as a rigorous engineering and scientific problem rather than a pre-processing step.

Scope
Signals

Signal theory, transforms, filtering, stochastic structure, and waveform analysis across serious sensing problems.

Multimodal Sensing

Cross-sensor observation, fused representations, missing-modality handling, and synchronized measurement systems.

Physiological Systems

Human physiological dynamics, multiscale regulation, and the interpretation of biosignals as partial observations of coupled systems.
